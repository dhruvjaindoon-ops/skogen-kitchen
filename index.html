Our Philosophy

Two Californians, one small town, and a quiet conviction that great food belongs everywhere.

Joseph and Eliza Raney left Southern California for a town of a few thousand people in the heart of the Black Hills. They came with a simple idea: that the warmth of fine dining shouldn't be reserved for big cities.

Everything begins with what's near — South Dakota growers, ranchers, and the seasons themselves — then travels far, drawing on techniques and flavors collected across a career in kitchens around the world.

The Chef

Cooking the world from a corner of Custer.

Chef Joseph Raney built Skogen around a single counter and an open kitchen, where dinner is less a transaction than a conversation. The menu shifts with what's good — foie gras and huckleberry one night, diver scallops with a Pedro Ximénez "caviar" the next.

Alongside GM Eliza Raney, the couple have turned a storefront on 5th Street into one of the region's most talked-about tables — recognized twice as a James Beard Award semifinalist, and still pouring the same warmth into every plate.

— Joseph & Eliza RaneyOwners · Skogen Kitchen
